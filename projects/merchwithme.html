MerchWithMe — Marketplace for University Clubs

Role: Co‑Developer & Researcher · Methods: Surveys, Interviews, Hypothesis Testing

Overview

MerchWithMe is a centralized online platform designed for Cambodian university clubs to simplify the process of ordering custom merchandise and event materials. The platform connects student organizations with trusted vendors, offering bulk discounts, customizable designs, and fast turnaround times — solving the long-standing challenge of fragmented, time-consuming procurement.

Through in-depth market research and surveys, the project identified three key needs of university clubs: affordable bulk orders, seasonal demand planning, and quick delivery options.

Problem

  • Limited budgets and fragmented vendor networks.
  • Time-consuming coordination between multiple suppliers.
  • Quality inconsistencies and slow deliveries.

These factors make it difficult for clubs to maintain brand visibility, support events, and engage members effectively.

Approach & Methods

  • Market Research: Collected data from academic literature, government reports, and club leader surveys to understand purchasing behavior, seasonal trends, and vendor challenges.
  • Customer Lab Experiments: Validated three core hypotheses:
    1. Bulk Order Discounts → 70% of clubs would order more frequently with tiered discounts.
    2. Seasonal Demand Peaks → Highest merchandise demand occurs at semester starts and major events.
    3. Quick Turnaround Times → 75% of clubs consider fast delivery critical, with many willing to pay extra.
  • Analysis: Combined qualitative thematic coding with quantitative survey statistics to guide feature priorities.

Results & Impact

  • Feature Prioritization: Built platform features around bulk discounts, seasonal promotions, and expedited delivery options.
  • Operational Strategy: Established vendor partnerships to ensure consistent quality and fast production.
  • Market Readiness: Positioned the platform to initially capture 5–10% of the annual campus merchandise spend, with potential growth through partnerships and targeted marketing.
